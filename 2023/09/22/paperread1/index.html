Paper Reading 1 - A Principled Approach to Selective Context Sensitivity for Pointer Analysis

⭐ Only for self-study and revision summaries

Basics (JAVA)

▲ Context Sensitivity
▲ Pointer Analysis & Static Analyses
· 2-object-sensitive highly-precise conventional context-sensitive pointer analysis (2-object-sensitive with a context-sensitive heap, 2obj for short)

Background

▲ Existing techniques are based on heuristics that do not provide much insight into what characterizes the subset.
▲ Context sensitivity has high accuracy, but efficiency costs too much and consumes too much space and time.

Related Work (Innovation & Extensions)

▲ Section 2: A short literature review and current Context-Sensitive Pointer Analysis Java trend dscription

▲ Section 3: 3 general patterns of value flow that explain how and where most imprecision is introduced in context-insensitive pointer analysis

▲ Section 4 & Section 5 (core parts):
· Section 4: Zipper
Effectively identifies 3 general patterns of value flow and thus identifies precision-critical approaches that benefit from context sensitivity
→ It makes pointer analysis run faster while maintaining most accuracy.

· Section 5: Zipper → Zipper express [Zippere(e)]
But the cost of analysis is additionally considered when choosing a method for context-sensitive analysis.
→ It makes pointer analysis run faster while maintaining higher accuracy.

· Both Zipper and Zippere(e) require opaque machine learning or other tweaks to multiple complex analysis parameters.

▲ Section 6: Extensive experiments evaluate the effectiveness of Zipper and Zipper(e) with RQ1, RQ2, RQ3, RQ4 and RQ5
· RQ1 → RQ4: Further investigate whether Zipper (and its underlying precision-loss principle) can preserve most of the precision also for other context-sensitivity variants
(Whether its precision loss pattern is common enough to effectively identify precision to preserve the accuracy of other mainstream context-sensitive variants, including call site sensitivity, type sensitivity, and mixed context sensitivity)

· RQ5: Evaluate the efficiency and accuracy of Zippere(e)
