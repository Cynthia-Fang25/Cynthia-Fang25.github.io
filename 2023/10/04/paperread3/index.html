▲ RIS (Reconfigurable Intelligent Surface) or IRS (Intelligent Reflection Surface)

Using a hypersurface composed of components with programmable characteristics, Which can adjust the amplitude, phase, and direction of transmission of electromagnetic waves, thus providing precise regulation and management of electromagnetic waves

· Applications
» 5G & 6G
i) A large number of test results show that the deployment of RIS can increase user throughput by 1 to 2 times, outdoor area edge coverage by 3 to 4 times, and indoor coverage by about 10 dB.
ii) RIS is expected to solve the coverage challenges of 5G millimetre wave and be applied in the terahertz band in the future as well.
iii) The maturity of RIS is closely related to the maturity of devices in different frequency bands.

Currently,
The maturity of devices in lower frequency bands (Sub-6G, millimetre wave low band) is high.
The maturity in millimetre wave high band and terahertz is low,
and it is difficult to fully realise the cost and energy advantages of RIS.

» Internet of Things (IoT)
Simultaneous Wireless Information and Power Transfer (SWIPT) technology transmits both signals and energy, and RIS can act like a relay, compensating for the huge energy consumption of long-distance transmission through passive beams, and helping to increase the wireless transmission power in the charging area.
» Large-scale D2D communication
» Physical layer-assisted secure communication
» Multi-stream transmission to increase the rank
» The new transceiver
RIS is real-time encodable, so the baseband signal is imported into the RIS controller in the form of encoding, and then the RF carrier in the target band is transmitted to the RIS, and the baseband signal can be modulated on top of the carrier through reflection.
» Coverage blindness eliminated
